The Peregrine Falcon: Fastest Animal Alive

The peregrine falcon is a bird of prey renowned for its extraordinary speed. During its characteristic hunting stoop, a high-speed dive from great height, it can exceed three hundred kilometres per hour, making it the fastest member of the animal kingdom by a wide margin.

Range and Habitat

Peregrines are found on every continent except Antarctica. They favour open landscapes and, increasingly, tall city buildings, where ledges mimic the cliff faces they nest on in the wild and where feral pigeons provide an abundant and reliable source of prey throughout the year.

Conservation

Populations collapsed during the mid twentieth century because of the pesticide DDT, which thinned eggshells and caused breeding failure. After DDT was banned and captive breeding programmes released thousands of birds, the peregrine recovered and is no longer endangered.
